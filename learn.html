Learn the Japanese Abacus (Soroban)

Follow these simple steps to understand the Soroban system and its use in arithmetic.

1. What Is the Soroban?

The Soroban is Japanâ€™s abacus with one upper bead and four lower beads per rod, used for arithmetic.

2. Structure

  • Top bead = 5
  • Bottom beads = 1 each
  • Middle bar = beam

3. Reading Numbers

Rightmost column = ones, next = tens, etc. Example: one top bead + two lower = 7.

4. Resetting

Push all beads away before starting new problems.

5. Calculations

Add by moving beads toward the beam; subtract by moving away. Carry or borrow as needed.

6. Mental Abacus

Advanced users visualize bead movements mentally to calculate instantly.

7. Hand Position

  • Thumb moves lower beads up
  • Index moves them down
  • Stay relaxed

8. Common Mistakes

  • Not clearing before start
  • Wrong place values

9. Benefits

  • Improves focus
  • Boosts memory
  • Sharpens number sense

10. Keep Practicing

Practice daily and visualize beads mentally for speed and clarity.
