Learn the Japanese Abacus (Soroban)

Learn simple steps to calculate with speed and clarity.

1. What Is the Soroban?

The Soroban is Japan’s abacus. It has one upper bead (5) and four lower beads (1) per rod. You can perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

2. Structure

  • Top bead (heaven) = 5
  • Bottom beads (earth) = 1 each
  • Middle bar = counting bar

Beads count when touching the bar. Columns represent ones, tens, hundreds from right to left.

3. Reading Numbers

Rightmost column is ones, next is tens, and so on. Example: 1 top bead + 2 bottom beads = 7.

4. Resetting

Push all beads away from the bar before starting each problem. Some Sorobans have a lever to reset.

5. Calculations

Addition

Slide beads toward the bar. Carry over if total > 9.

Subtraction

Slide beads away from the bar. Borrow from next rod if needed.

Multiplication & Division

Use repeated addition and subtraction. Practice basic operations first.

6. Mental Abacus

Advanced users visualize bead movements in their mind to calculate quickly.

7. Hand Position

  • Thumb moves lower beads up
  • Index finger moves lower beads down
  • Upper beads: thumb up, index down
  • Keep shoulders relaxed and eyes steady

8. Common Mistakes

  • Not clearing before calculation
  • Mixing place values
  • Inconsistent finger technique
  • Forgetting top bead = 5

9. Benefits

  • Improves focus
  • Boosts memory
  • Strengthens number sense
  • Builds confidence

10. Keep Practicing

Practice 10–15 minutes daily. Visualize beads mentally for faster calculations.
